My thoughts on the personal ethics in class

Everyone discussing their personal ethics in this class was really interesting for me.
A lot of people attributed some of the ten commandments like “Thou shalt not kill” and “Thou shalt not steal”
directly into the foundations of their personal ethical system, yet didn’t quote
“Thou shalt not bear false witness against thy neighbor”, the commandment associated with be honest to others,
despite most people valuing honesty among others as another strong ethical pillar.

I also thought it was weird how music or video games weren’t mentioned as much as I thought they would be
for a class full of CompSci majors discussing why their ethics are the way they are.
I would believe at least the type of video games someone plays would influence their personality and ethics
at such an impressionable age, but I myself either do not recognize it in me and my ethical system,
or it really isn't that influential in someone’s ethics.

In retrospect, I regret not bringing up my ethnic background, as I feel like it is at least an important factor in introducing myself,
including my own ethical structure I live day-to-day with. I don't think my ethnic background is that unique, but it
is at the very least different than most.
