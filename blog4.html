My Experience through Hurricane Helene


I had quite a strange time through all of the craziness of the hurricane. The Wednesday before the storm,
I had to go to the emergency room for my acting up back. Luckily I was able to get a prescription for muscle relaxant and pain suppressors,
but that means that during the brunt of the storm, I was on a prescribed daze watching the trees ourside my window do the equivalent of "the limbo".
I did lose both power and cellular connection so all I could really do was light some candles and rest my back, so the lack of technology
did not really affect me since I wasn't at will to use technology anyways.



The Sunday after the hurricane, I was finally able to go to work and my work miraculously survived with very minimal flooding in the back.
Even the internet was fully operational and I was able to contact a ton of people and be able to talk to coworkers about our personal experiences.
Everyone was sharing their stories and what they were able to film during the hurricane, and it was mind blowing and so surreal seeing places
I knew just get ravaged by crazy wind and flooding. Most of the following weeks was a blur of going to work, loading more news and contacting and updating everyone,
and then going home and doing chores while still taking prescribed pain suppressants for my back.
Im just so thankful that my situation was relatively calm.
